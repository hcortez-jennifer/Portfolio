La Catrina

Year: 2025

Category: Restaurant

Services: Brand identity / visual identity / web design / web development

Live site

Who is La Catrina?

La Catrina, a Mexican restaurant that's more than just a place to eat. It's a movement, a vibe, and an experience. Designed to break away from the ordinary, it is a place where flavors, music, and artisitic expression collide to create an immersive experience like no other. Inspired by the fearless spirit of La Catrina, a symbol of cultural pride, they are bold, expressive, and welcoming.

Design goals

The goal was to design a website that captures La Catrina's bold, artistic, and playful energy.

  1. Vivid, engaging color palette and patterns.
  2. Strong visual identity that merges Mexican culture with modern and artistic aesthetic.
  3. Seamless user experience for easy navigation, menu exploration, and event updates.
  4. Optimized for all devices to ensure responsiveness.

Logo

The La Catrina logo reflects their bold, playful, and soulful personality. I refined the letterforms, "C" and "N" to flow harmoniously with the organic swirls of the type, ensuring a smooth visual rhythm. The first "A" in Catrina was customized to resemble a sugar skull nose to honor Mexico's heritage. "Comida Con Alma" (Food With Soul), captures the brand's mission to serve not just meals, but emotional, art-driven experiences.

Colors

The colors for La Catrina were selected to convey their vibrant, bold, and energetic nature while maintaining their artistic touch. The combination of warm, energetic tones and contrasting hues ensures the design is both exciting and playful.

  1. Primary Color #1 (#CB6452) – Quiet Coral: A grounded hue that provides visual balance without becoming overwhelming. Used for backgrounds.
  2. Primary Color #2 (#F3E9DB) – Linen A neutral hue that allow the colors to be visually balanced and make them pop.
  3. Secondary Color #1 (#836496) – Velvet Dusk: A bold accent that adds contrast, excitement, and elegance. Used for borders.
  4. Secondary Color #2 (#FFE0AD) – Navajato white: Ensures an inviting and friendly tone. Used for readability, borders, and accents.
  5. Accent Color (#FF5733) – Tomato: Used for call-to-actions to add attention.
